## What I learned

- A **Region** is a geographic area (e.g. `af-south-1` Cape Town, `us-west-2` Oregon); each contains multiple isolated **Availability Zones (AZs)**.
- AZs are physically separate data centers within a Region — spreading resources across AZs gives high availability, so if one AZ fails another keeps serving traffic.
- **Edge Locations** are CDN endpoints (CloudFront) that cache content close to users for low latency — different from AZs, which run your compute.
- Rule of thumb: pick a Region close to your users (and check pricing/service availability) and design across multiple AZs for resilience.
